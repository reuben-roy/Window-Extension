# Window Investor Deck

Founder-but-polished investor deck for a pre-launch / prototype product.

Date prepared: April 12, 2026

## Slide 1 - Cover

**Window**

Protect deep work without losing ideas.

Focus infrastructure for ambitious people working in the browser.

Speaker note:
Window starts as a calendar-aware focus product and expands into a broader productivity operating layer. The opening frame should feel emotionally resonant but still credible.

## Slide 2 - Problem

**Dreams rarely die in one moment. They erode through tiny context switches.**

- The founder tracked his own work patterns for 90 days and found a common failure mode: attention fragments faster than progress compounds.
- Browser work is interruption-heavy because work, communication, research, and distraction all live in one place.
- Existing tools split the workflow: blockers stop sites, note apps store ideas, planners rearrange calendars, but none protect the whole loop.
- Good ideas become bad interruptions. Users either chase the thought and break focus, or suppress it and lose the idea.

Speaker note:
Use the founder's story here, but keep it sharp. The point is not self-help. The point is that the founder had a measurable insight into a real and recurring workflow failure.

## Slide 3 - Why Now

**Three shifts make this category newly winnable.**

- Work collapsed into the browser.
- AI now makes async idea processing feasible.
- Mainstream users still will not self-host complicated automation stacks.

Key line:
The gap in the market is between blockers that only say "no" and AI work suites that never touch the moment of distraction.

## Slide 4 - Product

**Window turns a calendar block into a protected work environment.**

- Connect Google Calendar.
- Detect the active event in real time.
- Apply an event-specific allowlist of domains.
- Block unrelated sites while the session is live.
- Let the user take an intentional 5, 10, or 15 minute break without turning the system off.
- Reinforce consistency with task progress and points.

Speaker note:
Treat this as real product, not concept art. The current codebase supports the extension flow and the backend scaffolding.

## Slide 5 - Magic Moment

**The breakthrough is not just blocking distraction. It is preserving valuable interruption.**

- During a focus session, the user gets a useful idea.
- They capture it in one sentence from the extension popup.
- Window queues it in the background and returns the user to work immediately.
- Later, Window returns a report with viability, competition, effort, risks, and suggested next steps.

Speaker note:
This is the core differentiator. It raises willingness to pay above a basic blocker and creates the bridge into the broader assistant roadmap.

## Slide 6 - Market

**Start with the people who feel this pain most sharply, then expand outward.**

- Beachhead: solo builders, founders, creators, and engineers with scheduled deep work.
- Expansion: high-output professionals, freelancers, consultants, and serious students.
- Team wedge: startup teams and consultancies through bottom-up adoption.
- Longer term: privacy-first and enterprise deployments once admin controls and local-model options exist.

Key line:
This is not a generic "everyone gets distracted" product. The beachhead is ambitious people already trying to protect deep work.

## Slide 7 - Competition

**Window sits between blockers, trackers, and AI planners.**

- Freedom, Cold Turkey, and Opal are strong at blocking and habit interruption.
- RescueTime and Rize are strong at measurement and retrospective visibility.
- Motion is strong at AI planning and calendar orchestration.
- Window's position is browser-native focus control plus async idea preservation.

Positioning statement:
Window is the execution layer between planning work and actually doing it.

## Slide 8 - Competitive Strategy

**Win with precision, low friction, and a compounding product loop.**

- Do not fight for "strictest blocker."
- Win on calendar precision and event-specific context.
- Use browser-native distribution and setup simplicity as a wedge.
- Use idea capture and assistant behavior to expand value beyond blocking.
- Add privacy-first local models, recommendations, and team controls over time.

Key line:
The defensibility path is not "we block harder." It is "we become the default interface for focused execution in the browser."

## Slide 9 - Go-to-Market

**Start bottom-up with one sharp promise: get your first protected focus session in minutes.**

- Use the Chrome Web Store as the primary distribution wedge.
- Drive acquisition through founder-led content built from the 90-day focus story and build-in-public product narrative.
- Launch into Product Hunt, maker communities, productivity communities, and developer audiences.
- Optimize activation around completed protected sessions, not installs.

Speaker note:
The first success metric is not traffic. It is whether the user reaches a protected focus session fast enough to feel the product "click."

## Slide 10 - Scale Plan

**Retention deepens first. Expansion follows the users who already trust Window with their work.**

- Saved rules, points, streaks, and historical idea reports create compounding retention.
- Referrals and accountability features can create lightweight social loops.
- Team adoption should come from power-user pull, not enterprise-first selling.
- Enterprise becomes credible later through privacy controls and local-model deployment options.

## Slide 11 - Business Model

**Price Window above commodity blockers and below full AI work suites.**

### Free

- Calendar connection
- Basic blocking
- Limited rules
- Standard breaks
- Enough functionality to prove value fast

### Pro - $12 / month or $96 / year

- Unlimited rules
- Keyword matching
- Points and streaks
- Idea capture
- Limited monthly AI reports
- Analytics and recommendations as they ship

### Team - $15 / user / month annual

- Shared policies
- Pooled AI usage
- Admin reporting
- Onboarding
- Billing controls

### Enterprise later

- Custom pricing
- SSO
- Privacy controls
- Local-model deployment options

### Pricing justification

- Blockers alone are cheap and easy to compare.
- Window should not be sold as a commodity blocker because it blends focus control with assistant value.
- Pro is priced above basic focus tools and below higher-end AI work suites.
- AI usage should be limited in Pro to protect margins and keep the tier economically sound.

### Official benchmark pricing viewed April 12, 2026

- Freedom Premium: $8.99 / month or $99.50 lifetime
- RescueTime Solo: $7 / month annual
- RescueTime Solo+: $12 / month annual
- Opal Pro web program: $8.29 / month billed annually
- Rize Basic: $9.99 / month billed annually
- Motion Pro AI: $19 / seat / month

## Slide 12 - Close

**Window is not another blocker. It is focus infrastructure for ambitious people.**

- The wedge is simple: protect deep work in the browser.
- The expansion path is meaningful: preserve ideas, layer in recommendations, then automation.
- The long-term opportunity is to become the browser-native productivity operating system for people who want to finish what matters.

Closing line:
If we can protect the hour where someone's real work happens, we earn the right to help with everything around it.
